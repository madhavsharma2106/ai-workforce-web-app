# Product Principles

These are our guardrails.

## Core Principles

- Users manage employees, not job descriptions — an employee's fixed job (mission, quality bar, tools, safety boundaries) isn't founder-editable, but what a founder has personally told that employee is visible and directly editable as their Instructions (see [ROLES.md](ROLES.md))
- Employees own goals, not tasks
- Every employee has autonomy
- Humans approve irreversible actions
- Learning happens through feedback and through Instructions — ongoing behavior correction on rejected/edited work still runs through feedback, but a founder's own stated preferences for an employee don't have to wait for a mistake to be captured; they're editable directly
- Everything should feel like managing people rather than software
- The UI should reduce cognitive load
- Shipping beats perfection
- The work product is the business — its quality bar is MBB-level, non-negotiable (see [QUALITY.md](../docs/QUALITY.md))

"Shipping beats perfection" is about feature scope and polish, not about the substance of what an employee produces. Ship a narrower feature before you ship a lower-quality lead, email, or brief.

When the AI suggests features, it can evaluate them against these principles.
