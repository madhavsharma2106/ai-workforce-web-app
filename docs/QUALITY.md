# Quality Bar

At the core, this is a services business. The product is the delivery mechanism — the work product (research, drafts, briefs) is what's actually being sold.

Every employee's output must meet the bar of a top-tier consulting engagement (MBB: McKinsey, Bain, BCG), not "good enough AI output."

## What MBB-level means here

- **Structured, not dumped.** Findings are organized around the founder's decision, not a raw list of everything found.
- **Evidence-backed.** Every claim traces to a source (a company's site, a filing, a past interaction) — no invented specifics.
- **Tailored, not templated.** Output reflects this specific founder's ICP, tone, and priorities — not a generic template with names swapped in.
- **Concise.** Say the minimum that carries the point. No filler, no hedging, no restating the question.
- **Defensible.** Every output should survive a manager asking "why?" — the reasoning is one layer beneath the conclusion, ready if asked.

## Where this shows up

- **Lead Sourcer** — qualification reasoning and email drafts must read like they were written by someone who actually researched the company, not filled from a mail-merge. See [roles/lead-sourcer.md](../roles/lead-sourcer.md).
- **Account Manager** — the Business Profile must be precise enough that another employee could act on it without re-asking the client basic questions. See [roles/account-manager.md](../roles/account-manager.md).

## What this is not

- Not a license to over-build UI polish or add scope — see [MVP.md](MVP.md) and [ANTI_GOALS.md](ANTI_GOALS.md). The bar applies to the substance of the work product, not to feature surface area or visual polish.
- Not a request for longer output. MBB-quality is often shorter than the alternative.

## Enforcing it

- Treat feedback/rejection on any employee's output as a quality-bar signal, not just a one-off correction — see each role's `Experience` inputs in `roles/*.md`.
- If shipping a feature would force a quality tradeoff on the actual work product (a leads list, an email, a brief), don't ship it that way — narrow scope instead, don't lower the bar.
